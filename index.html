Wave-based Extraction and Selection for Longitudinal Data

About this package

weasel is an R package for selecting, filtering, and balancing longitudinal panel data across survey waves. It addresses a common problem in panel research: respondents enter late, skip waves, or drop out entirely, and this missingness must be handled systematically before any model can be fit.

Rather than requiring researchers to write ad hoc subsetting code, weasel provides two structured pipelines — one for exploratory pattern inspection, one for scenario-based selection — along with tools for generating reproducible methods-section prose.

Scope pipeline
Interactively explore the wave-presence patterns in your data and extract subsets matching specific participation fingerprints.
set_weasel_scope ➜ reshape_to_wide ➜ summarize_waves ➜ get_data_by_row
Plan pipeline
Compare scenario-based selection rules and choose the best balance between sample size, coverage, and structural completeness.
weasel_plan ➜ weasel_compare_scenarios ➜ weasel_apply ➜ weasel_justify_subset

The decision guide below walks you through a sequence of substantive choices — data source, analytic goal, wave range, scenario strategy — and delivers a concrete R workflow at the end. Each step explains not only what to choose, but why the distinction matters analytically.
